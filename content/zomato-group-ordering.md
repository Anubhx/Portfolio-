# Why this mattered

I lived this problem.

Every office lunch required:

WhatsApp polls.

Restaurant debates.

Manual order collection.

UPI reminders.

Awkward payment conversations.

Lunch coordination often took longer than eating lunch itself.

---

# The Problem

Food wasn't the problem.

Coordination was.

Zomato's ordering flow assumes:

• One person

• One restaurant

• One payment

Real group behaviour looked nothing like that.

---

# Research

Five interviews were conducted with Bengaluru professionals across Product, Engineering, Finance, Operations, and Research roles.

The same themes appeared repeatedly:

People hated chasing payments.

Teams wanted food from multiple restaurants.

Managers struggled with sponsored meal budgets.

Nobody wanted another app.

---

# Key Insights

Social friction outweighs functional friction.

Trust collapses during payment.

Most users value convenience over unlimited choice.

Familiarity drives adoption.

---

# Competitive Analysis

Neither Zomato nor Swiggy supports:

• Multi-restaurant ordering

• Built-in split payments

• Budget enforcement

This represented a significant opportunity.

---

# Information Architecture

The selected model:

Distributed Cart.

Everyone owns their order.

The host confirms the group.

Payments happen transparently.

---

# Solution

The redesigned experience introduced:

• Group creation

• Invite links

• Individual ownership

• Split payments

• Budget controls

• Unified delivery tracking

---

# Outcomes

Coordination time reduced:

25 minutes → 9 minutes.

Task success improved:

83% → 96%.

Payment follow-ups eliminated.

Participants understood the experience immediately.

---

# Reflection

The hardest problems aren't technical.

They're emotional.

People weren't afraid of paying.

They were afraid of being difficult.

Designing for relationships changed how I approached collaboration forever.
